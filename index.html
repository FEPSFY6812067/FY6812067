Abstract

      This paper provides an overview of robotic surgery, following its evolution from the initial 
    robotic surgical arm in the mid-1980s to the sophisticated 'da VinciTM' system widely used today.
    Key turning points are included in the historical course such as the PUMA 560, AESOP 1000, and ZEUS,
    leading up to the FDA-certified da Vinci in 2000. It will also explore and discuss the different parts 
    that the da Vinci surgical systems consists of and the way they work together to operate on the patient
    with the help of the surgeon. Furthermore, the advantages of robotic surgery, such as precision, enhanced 
    visualization, and quicker recovery, are highlighted, alongside disadvantages like high costs, technical 
    malfunctions, and limited accessibility.
